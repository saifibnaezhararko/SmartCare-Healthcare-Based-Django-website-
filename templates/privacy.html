Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 5, 2024

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

At SmartCare, we are committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal and medical information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our healthcare services.

We comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), GDPR where applicable, and other relevant privacy laws to ensure your health information remains confidential and secure.

Information We Collect

1. Personal Information:

  • Name, date of birth, gender, and contact information (address, phone, email)
  • Government-issued identification numbers (e.g., Social Security Number, ID card)
  • Insurance information and payment details
  • Emergency contact information
  • Employment and demographic information when relevant to your care

2. Medical Information:

  • Medical history, including past diagnoses, treatments, and procedures
  • Current medications, allergies, and immunization records
  • Laboratory and diagnostic test results
  • Clinical notes and physician observations
  • Mental health information and counseling records
  • Genetic information when relevant to your care

3. Technical Information:

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Website usage data and navigation patterns
  • Login credentials and account activity
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

Healthcare Operations:

  • Providing medical treatment, consultations, and healthcare services
  • Coordinating care between healthcare providers
  • Managing appointments and sending reminders
  • Processing prescriptions and laboratory orders
  • Quality improvement and patient safety initiatives

Administrative Purposes:

  • Processing payments and billing insurance companies
  • Verifying insurance coverage and benefits
  • Managing patient accounts and medical records
  • Responding to patient inquiries and requests
  • Conducting internal audits and compliance reviews

Legal and Safety:

  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Responding to court orders, subpoenas, and legal processes
  • Preventing fraud and protecting against security threats
  • Reporting required public health information
  • Addressing urgent health and safety situations

Communication:

  • Sending appointment reminders and test results
  • Providing health education and wellness information
  • Notifying you about changes to our services or policies
  • Conducting patient satisfaction surveys (with your consent)

Information Sharing and Disclosure

SmartCare does not sell your personal or medical information. We only share your information in the following circumstances:

With Your Consent:

  • When you provide explicit authorization to share information with third parties
  • For marketing and fundraising purposes (you may opt out at any time)

Healthcare Providers:

  • With physicians, specialists, and healthcare professionals involved in your care
  • With laboratories, pharmacies, and diagnostic facilities
  • With hospitals and care facilities for coordinated treatment
  • With referral providers as needed for specialist consultations

Business Associates:

  • With service providers who perform functions on our behalf (billing, IT services)
  • All business associates sign agreements to protect your information

Legal Requirements:

  • When required by law or court order
  • To public health authorities for disease prevention and reporting
  • To law enforcement in specific circumstances (e.g., suspected abuse)
  • For workers' compensation or disability claims
  • To coroners, medical examiners, and funeral directors when necessary

Health and Safety:

  • To prevent serious threats to health or safety
  • For organ and tissue donation purposes
  • For research purposes (de-identified data only, unless you provide authorization)

How We Protect Your Information

SmartCare implements comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Secure Socket Layer (SSL) technology for website communications
  • Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Multi-factor authentication for system access

Physical Safeguards:

  • Secure facilities with controlled access
  • Video surveillance and security personnel
  • Locked storage for physical records
  • Secure disposal of documents and electronic media

Administrative Safeguards:

  • Comprehensive privacy and security policies
  • Regular staff training on privacy and security practices
  • Access controls limiting who can view your information
  • Incident response procedures for potential breaches
  • Regular review and updates of security measures

Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal and medical information:

Access Rights:

  • Right to access and review your medical records
  • Right to request copies of your health information
  • Right to receive information in an electronic format when possible

Amendment Rights:

  • Right to request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information
  • We may deny requests if information is accurate and complete
  • If denied, you may submit a statement of disagreement

Restriction Rights:

  • Right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures
  • Right to request restrictions on information shared with insurance companies (if you pay out-of-pocket in full)
  • We may decline requests that interfere with healthcare operations or legal obligations

Communication Rights:

  • Right to request confidential communications (e.g., alternative phone numbers)
  • Right to opt out of appointment reminders and marketing communications

Accounting Rights:

  • Right to receive an accounting of disclosures we have made
  • Exceptions apply for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations

Notification Rights:

  • Right to be notified in the event of a breach of your information
  • We will notify you within 60 days of discovering a breach

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality and security
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analytics: We use analytics tools to improve our services

You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences. Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

Children's Privacy

SmartCare provides pediatric healthcare services and collects medical information from children with parental or guardian consent. Parents and legal guardians have the right to:

  • Access their child's medical records
  • Request corrections to their child's information
  • Control the disclosure of their child's health information
  • Receive notifications about their child's care

Minors' rights may vary based on state laws regarding consent for specific healthcare services.

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites for your convenience and information. SmartCare is not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

SmartCare reserves the right to update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will:

  • Post the updated policy on our website with a new "Last Updated" date
  • Notify you of material changes via email or posted notice
  • Make previous versions available upon request

Your continued use of our services after policy changes indicates acceptance of the updated terms.
